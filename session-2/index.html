Section 02 · Golden Rules

Four rules that always apply

  • Be specific. Vague in → vague out.
  • Give context. The AI can't read your mind.
  • Ask for a format. Table, bullets, word count, step-by-step.
  • Always fact-check anything that actually matters.

"Vague in.
Vague out."

Section 03 · The Habit

When the answer's not quite right

  • Close the chat and start a brand-new prompt from scratch
  • Push back in the same thread — the AI already has the context

Iteration is faster than repetition, every time.

Section 03 · The Follow-Up Moves

Five moves that fix almost anything

shorter."

"Explain point 3

"Give me a

Five moves. Memorize them — they cover 90% of bad first answers.

Section 03 · The Habit That Compounds

Keep a prompt journal

  • Save prompts that actually worked — don't rebuild them from memory next time
  • Reuse and refine them across projects
  • Your journal becomes a personal library of proven recipes

A working prompt saved once is worth ten rewritten from scratch.

LAB · 01

Exercise 1 — prompt makeover

  • Take your 3 weak prompts from the break
  • Rewrite each using the 5 ingredients — Role, Context, Task, Format, Examples
  • Run both versions. Compare the outputs side by side
10–12 min. Ask 2–3 people to share their before/after out loud.
LAB · 01 · Before / After

Makeover in action

before → after
before: "help with my essay"

after:
"Act as a strict writing coach.
This is a 500-word college application essay
on overcoming a challenge, due tomorrow.
Point out the 3 weakest sentences and tell me
exactly why, in a numbered list."
Use this as the template if no one volunteers their own yet.
LAB · 02

Exercise 2 — head-to-head

  • Pair up. Same goal for both: "a study plan for next week's math test"
  • Each person writes their own prompt, independently
  • Compare results — then merge the best parts of both
10 min. The merge step is the real lesson: no single prompt is ever perfect.
LAB · 03

Exercise 3 — format flex

  • Take one good answer from earlier
  • Ask AI to reformat it: table → summary → quiz questions → tweet-length
  • Notice what each format loses and what it gains

Same content, four shapes — format is a lever, not an afterthought.

10 min. This drives home that Format is its own ingredient, not a nice-to-have.
